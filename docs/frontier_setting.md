# JOJIFrontier Scenario Setting

## Status and canon boundary

This is an engine-local scenario proposal for JOJIFrontier. Shared facts about
Asteria and Embermarch remain owned by `../../JojiWorldBible`. This document may
specialize a playable expedition, outpost, and cast, but it must not define what
ultimately exists beyond the March Line without a deliberate World Bible update.

## Premise

JOJIFrontier takes place on Embermarch's southern frontier. A chain of minor
watch posts beyond the main March Line has gone silent after an ash storm. The
Warmarch cannot weaken the whole frontier to investigate, so small four-person
expedition teams are sent from a reclaimed staging post called **Cinderwatch**.

The expeditions recover maps, tools, medicine, iron, and watch records from the
lost posts. Nothing belongs to the player merely because it was found. Supplies
become permanent property only after the party returns safely to Cinderwatch.

The first playable region deliberately does not reveal the ancient threat beyond
the March Line. Early enemies are human: deserters, smugglers, rival salvage
crews, and armed groups using abandoned posts. Strange tracks and damaged
fortifications can foreshadow the larger mystery without defining its answer.

## Player role

The player is the **Expedition Marshal** of Cinderwatch. This is an operational
appointment, not a noble title and not a replacement for the Warmarch. The
marshal chooses a four-person team, provisions it, selects routes, and decides at
each camp whether to press farther or return with secured gains.

The player does not gain levels. Progress belongs to Cinderwatch:

- **Forge**: weapon refits and alternate weapon traits.
- **Drill Yard**: class techniques and formation actions.
- **Chart Room**: route intelligence, enemy previews, and safer starting tiles.
- **Barracks**: roster and four-person party composition.
- **Storehouse**: expedition capacity and protection from partial loss.

Regional bosses provide the key objects needed for major facility upgrades:
command seals, intact survey instruments, forge dies, bridge gear, and lost
garrison ledgers. These are practical frontier assets, not arbitrary crystals.

## Initial four roles

Names remain uncommitted until the cast is added to the World Bible or explicitly
approved as scenario-local characters.

| Role | Battlefield identity | Exploration choice |
|---|---|---|
| March Captain | formation support and balanced front line | negotiate with soldiers and read orders |
| Veteran Guard | Zone of Control and lane protection | force damaged gates and guard civilians |
| Watch Archer | long range and no adjacent attack | reveal encounters and choose deployment |
| Dawn Chirurgeon | adjacent healing and limited magical attack | inspect wounds, herbs, and old medical stores |

Magic in Asteria is learned and uncommon; the Dawn Chirurgeon's presence
in a four-person expedition must feel like a valuable specialist assignment.

## First expedition: The Silent Posts

1. **Cinderwatch Gate**: tutorial battle against looters inside a damaged post.
2. **Split Ash Road**: text choice between a direct road, a collapsed service
   trench, or an Archer scouting option.
3. **Ironwatch Stores**: recover medicine or weapon stock; taking both increases
   the next encounter's enemy strength.
4. **Cold Fire Camp**: continue or return with pending loot.
5. **The Last Signal**: reclaim a signal tower held by a former garrison captain.

The regional boss is human and mechanically tests lane control and attack-range
warnings. Defeating that captain restores the signal chain and unlocks the Chart
Room. It does not answer what damaged the farther posts.

## Tone

Dialogue should be terse, practical, and proverb-like. Characters discuss roads,
watches, food, steel, weather, and what can be carried home. Avoid modern banter
and ornate high-fantasy exposition.

Example lines:

- "A found blade feeds no forge until it crosses the gate."
- "The line held. The posts did not. Find the difference."
- "Four go farther than forty when forty must hold the wall."

## Visual identity

Embermarch's canonical colors are Ember Red, Iron Black, and Ash Gray. Use them
for banners and HUD accents, not to recolor every battlefield tile. Battlefield
floors stay neutral so movement and attack overlays remain legible.
